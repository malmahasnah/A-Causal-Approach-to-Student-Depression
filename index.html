Introduction and Motivation

Mental health disorders have always been and continue to be a major concern in people's lives. In the US, at least 28% of college students were diagnosed with depression or another mood disorder. Additionally, more than 9 in 10 students who face academic challenges say these challenges affect their mental health. The goal of our project is to use causal knowledge to establish a framework for the root causes of depression, specifically in students.

Introduction

One of the main difficulties in understanding depression is differentiating its causes and its outcomes. The main goal of the project is to understand the causal relationships between certain factors and depression, and in doing so laying a framework as to what factors are most important to focus on in order to prevent the onset of depression. The data we are using contains a variety of factors about students and their lives. These data points include, but are not limited to, their quantity of sleep, financial stress level, academic pressure, academic success, their current level of schooling and whether or not they are depressed. This data will work well for our goal, as much of the data is very much under the control of the students. Focusing our efforts on data such as dietary habits and profession can allow us to help students make more informed decisions. Ultimately helping them better understand how to combat depression by identifying potential risk factors, seeking support systems as appropriate, and most importantly taking measures to target specific lifestyle factors to prevent depression. Past studies such as "Causal Factors of Anxiety and Depression in College Students: Longitudinal Ecological Momentary Assessment and Causal Analysis Using PCMCI" have attempted to utilize methods such as the Peter and Clark algorithm to identify significant factors. While this approach is close to ours, we will also use the Fast KCI method for non-linear dependencies and incorporate counterfactual policy evaluation to investigate how changes in certain variables could impact depression outcomes. In addition to this, we will incorporate more modifiable lifestyle factors, as opposed to ones they focused on such as self-esteem.
